1\) Weapon Rush — Redline Kill Chain

Fantasy



Plays like a knife fight at engine-burn speed.

You mark a weakness, force a firing lane open, then unload enough burst that the opponent never gets to stabilize.



Key cards

Breach Mark

Paint Target

Pulse Tap

Critical Lock Cell

Synergy chain

Breach Mark or Paint Target applies Expose.

Pulse Tap becomes efficient because exposed targets replace themselves with draw.

Critical Lock Cell turns the next heavy weapon into a finisher.

Follow with Charged Shot, Piercing Beam, or Finisher Torpedo for the close.

When the order is right



You curve mark → pressure → amplifier → finisher and threaten lethal by turns 6–8.



When the order is wrong



It still functions as a clean midrange burn deck.

Your cards are individually fine; you just lose some damage efficiency and card flow.



20-card decklist

2x	Paint Target

2x	Pulse Tap

2x	Finisher Torpedo

2x	Charged Shot

2x	Piercing Beam

2x	Amplifier Cell

2x	Critical Lock Cell

2x	Afterburn

2x	Tactical Predict

Win condition



Stick Expose, then chain 2–3 high-efficiency weapon turns before the opponent’s defense engine comes online.



Weakness



Heavy Block and damage denial. If the opponent survives the first burst window, this deck starts trading down.



Best ship class



fighter



2\) Drone Swarm — Hangar Flood

Fantasy



Feels like opening every bay door and letting the void fill with friendly fire signatures.

You do not win with one hit. You win because the board never stops shooting.



Key cards

Carrier Deploy

Drone Controller

Fleet Sync

Tyrant Raider Wing / Escort Wing

Synergy chain

Carrier Deploy or early utility drones establish the board.

Drone Controller gives newly deployed drones Hot Deploy and Twin Barrels on entry turns.

Fleet Sync turns all those ticks into real pressure.

Paint Target or Predator Lock adds Drone Mark, making swarm fire more punishing.

When the order is right



The deck snowballs brutally: deploy board, buff board, mark target, and force the opponent to solve multiple damage sources at once.



When the order is wrong



It still works as a board-control shell.

Your drones buy time, block hits, and chip damage until the buffs arrive.

Win condition



Overwhelm the opponent with 3–5 active drone bodies and convert board presence into unavoidable tick damage.



Weakness



Wide anti-drone damage and tempo clears. If the board gets reset twice, the deck struggles to rebuild fast enough.



Best ship class



carrier



3\) Shield Fortress — Aegis Bastion

Fantasy



Feels patient, calm, and impossible to move.

You stack defense, let the enemy spend themselves against your wall, then win because they simply run out of meaningful threats.



Key cards

Shield Lattice

Persistent Barrier

Auto-Repair Protocol

Crash Override

Synergy chain

Open with cheap defense like Quick Shield and Deflector Net.

Transition into Shield Lattice and Persistent Barrier to retain value across turns.

Auto-Repair Protocol units or effects make removal inefficient.

Crash Override and long-game repair cards close the door on burst decks.

When the order is right



The opponent gets one early window, fails to break through, and then every later turn becomes worse for them.



When the order is wrong



It still functions because almost every card gains time.

You may not build the perfect fortress curve, but you rarely brick completely.



20-card decklist

2x	Repair Drone

2x	Patchwork Bastion

Win condition



Survive until the opponent’s deck is out of pressure, then win by attrition through reflected damage, passive healing, and superior card economy.



Weakness



Board-wide persistent damage and economic decks that do not care whether the first kill attempt works.



Best ship class



hauler



4\) ECM Control — Black Signal Lock

Fantasy



Feels like fighting with a hand on the opponent’s throat and the other in their command bus.

Instead of racing damage, you make their turns small, awkward, and late.



Key cards

Hunter Logic

Predator Lock

Timing Override

Needlefang Hunter

Synergy chain

Hunter Logic and Predator Lock stack Expose, Lock, and Drone Mark.

Timing Override or Vector Cut sets up Priority Fire windows.

Needlefang Hunter converts contact into Critical Breach, deleting drones and winning exchanges.

Jam follow-up from Signal Jam, EMP Drone, or Trace Protocol keeps the opponent off-script.

When the order is right



You establish status control early, deny a clean response, then pick apart drones and key threats before they resolve profitably.



When the order is wrong



It still plays as a disruption-midrange deck.

You may not fully lock the opponent, but the statuses still tax sequencing and buy time.



20-card decklist

Qty	Card

2x	Signal Jam

2x	Hunter Logic

2x	Predator Lock

2x	Timing Override

2x	Vector Cut

2x	Trace Protocol

2x	EMP Drone

2x	Needlefang Hunter

2x	Null Ray

2x	ECM Suite

Win condition



Assemble layered disruption so the opponent never gets a clean power turn, then finish with efficient weapons or drone trades they cannot answer well.



Weakness



Big defensive shells and recursive value engines. If the opponent is happy to play long and can cleanse statuses, your lock loses bite.



Best ship class



explorer



5\) Salvage Engine — Scrap Ascension

Fantasy



Feels slow, clever, and inevitable.

You turn wreckage into advantage, recycle key pieces, and eventually reach a point where every trade favors you.



Key cards

Salvage Drone

Salvage Harpoon

Scrap Injector

Scrapline Reclaimer

Synergy chain

Salvage Drone and Salvo Barrage start feeding Scrap.

Scrap Injector and Scrap Synergy convert that resource into defense and cards.

Scrapline Reclaimer brings back destroyed drones with System Reboot.

Archive Recall / Recycle Protocol keep the engine turning until late-game value crushes the table.

When the order is right



You stabilize the early game, establish scrap flow, then create a recursive resource loop the opponent cannot outgrind.



When the order is wrong



It still functions as a modest control-value deck.

You can block, cycle, and recover cards until the salvage pieces reconnect.



20-card decklist

2x	Salvage Harpoon

2x	Salvo Barrage

2x	Scrap Injector

2x	Scrap Synergy

Win condition



Survive long enough to turn every destroyed asset into more cards, more Scrap, and more board presence than the opponent can match.



Weakness



Fast decks that kill before Scrap matters, and swarm decks that force too many early answers at once.



Best ship class



salvager

Counter cards

Against Weapon Rush

Shield Lattice — retained Block forces rush to overcommit.

Crash Override — denies lethal swing turns.

Timing Override — steals the tempo window rush depends on.

Against Drone Swarm

EMP Nova — broad damage plus Jam hits swarm boards hard.

Hazard Pulse — efficient wide clear.

EMP Net — clean answer to a key buffed drone.

Against Shield Fortress

Shatter Beam — punishes Block-heavy lines.

Armor Strip Charge — reduces defensive scaling.

Swarm Intelligence — turns every drone into growing nontraditional pressure.

Against ECM Control

Hard Reboot — clears stacked statuses at once.

Coolant Purge — cheap cleanse for Burn/Jam/Lock.

Digital Firewall — protects against Jam-based disruption.

Against Salvage Engine

Finisher Torpedo — closes before recursion matters.

Drone Controller — swarm tempo pressures slow setup.

Chaff Packet — dilutes their draw quality and slows the loop.
