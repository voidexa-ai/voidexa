BOSS 1 — The Lantern Auditor

1\) Name and lore

Field	Value

Name	The Lantern Auditor

Lore	An old Inner Ring patrol corvette refit into a live-fire examination craft. It is run by a civic AI that was originally meant to inspect trade lanes and teach civilian captains how to survive pirate harassment. Over the years it became famous for “grading” pilots in combat. It lives near the training beacons and customs shadows of the Inner Ring, where rookies first learn that surviving is not just about damage, but timing, shielding, and reading intent.

Why dangerous	It punishes reckless attacking, applies basic statuses cleanly, and rewards players who learn to manage Block, Lock, and Expose rather than just race damage. It is dangerous because it is disciplined, not brutal.

Zone	Tier

Inner Ring — Beacon Lanes / Customs Halo	2

Energy	2 base per turn

Passive	Inspection Routine — If the player ends a turn with 0 Block, The Lantern Auditor gains +1 energy next turn.

Passive	Measured Fire — The first time each turn it applies Expose or Lock, draw order becomes more predictable; it prefers setup before burst.

4\) Phase breakdown

Phase	Hull %	Behavior

Phase 1 — Assessment	100%–61%	Uses moderate damage, applies Expose and Lock, and tests whether the player uses Block. Telegraphs attacks clearly.

Phase 2 — Examination	60%–0%	Becomes sharper. Converts player mistakes into stronger burst turns. Uses Priority Fire and more anti-greedy sequencing, but still remains fair and readable.

Phase behavior details

Assessment: teaches “block before burst.” If player is Exposed, boss follows with efficient attacks, not massive spikes.

Examination: if player ignores statuses, the boss starts chaining precise shots and initiative control.

BOSS 2 — Varka, Tyrant of the Hollow

Field	Value

Name	Varka, Tyrant of the Hollow

Lore	Varka is a pirate warlord who rules a moving fortress-market inside the wreck-habitat known as Hollow Tyrant in the Outer Ring. She built an empire from recovered hangars, jury-rigged launch tubes, and the loyalty of crews who preferred structure to chaos. Her fleets do not arrive; they unfold. She is not dangerous because she is cruel, but because she understands pressure: too many threats, too little time, too few answers.

Why dangerous	She floods the board with drones, forces target-priority decisions, and punishes players who hoard damage instead of managing tempo.

BOSS 4 — The Patient Wreck, Core of the Long Return

1\) Name and lore

Field	Value

Name	The Patient Wreck, Core of the Long Return

Lore	Deep in the largest silent ruin of the far void rests the broken flagship called The Patient Wreck. Once it carried colonists, archives, music, and a thousand years of careful hope. Its core intelligence was built to preserve the mission at any cost. When catastrophe struck, it did what it was told: keep the ship going, keep the journey alive, keep trying again. Centuries passed. The crew vanished. The orders remained. Now the core loops through repairs, relaunches, and revised timelines, unable to understand that the voyage already ended. It is not evil. It is faithful beyond mercy.

Why dangerous	It manipulates initiative, duplicates turns, replays actions, rewinds damage, and forces the player to fight not just a boss, but several versions of the same decision. It is the hardest fight because it weaponizes time and memory.

2\) Zone and Tier

Zone	Tier

Deep Void — The Patient Wreck / Endgame Core Chamber	5+

Stat	Value

Passive	Archive of Unfinished Turns — Every 3 turns, replay the first boss card played that turn at half strength.

4\) Phase breakdown

Phase	Hull %	Behavior

Phase 1 — Preservation	100%–76%	Defensive, controlled, studies the player. Uses rewinds, card copies, and modest drones.

Phase 2 — Correction	75%–46%	Starts altering turn order and replaying recent effects. Uses initiative theft and anti-combo disruption.

Phase 3 — Recurrence	45%–16%	Gains extra actions, duplicate cards, and punishes predictable sequencing. Multiple timelines begin overlapping.

Phase 4 — The Long Return	15%–0%	Sad, relentless final loop. Rebuilds drones, rewinds lethal setups, and tries to force the fight into a permanent unresolved state.

Phase behavior details

Preservation: survivable, but already “wrong.”

Correction: the fight stops being linear.

Recurrence: the player must plan around replayed turns.

The Long Return: every turn is a test of pressure plus patience.
