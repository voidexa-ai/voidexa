## PART 2 — EXPANDING UNIVERSE, LORE AND WORLD

### The core premise
voidexa is a star system. Each company that joins plants a planet. Each player is a pilot in the space between planets. The universe is neither fully a game nor fully a service — it is a shared 3D space where business and play happen in the same frame.

### The voidexa AI Cast (character bible)
Stored as `VOIDEXA_CAST.md`. Six characters, all playable as NPCs, boss voices, quest-givers, or chat personalities:

The cast is not cosmetic — it is the **voice layer** of the game. Quest-givers, boss dialogue, loading screen tips, arena announcer, shop merchant greetings. Each character has a color (voidexa cast memory: Jix amber, Claude blue, GPT green, Perplexity orange, Gemini purple, Llama gray).

**Quantum Live** is the weekly debate show where all six voices appear live with buzzer-based turn-taking, Whisper + TTS + KCP-90 pipeline, user voice always priority. Gamified content angle: players who have beaten a Quantum Live boss in PvE get a "weekly boss" slot in the next live show.

### Factions (world structure)
Not written down before — this is recovered and synthesized from scattered mentions of "planet ownership", "pioneer reward", "the board", "fuel your planet", and the Elite Dangerous-inspired universe.

1. **voidexa Core** — the central star. Not a faction in the PvP sense, but the rule-setter. Quantum, Void Chat, Trading Hub, Control Plane are the "core planets" that come free with the universe. Players are neutral with Core by default.

2. **Pioneer Planets** — the first 10 company-planet owners, hand-onboarded by Jix. They get 10M GHAI vesting over 18 months (5M instant + 5M over 18 months), the right to customize planet color, and a permanent "Pioneer" badge. In the gaming fiction they are lore-characters — "the First Ten" who founded the outer system.

3. **Claimed Planets** — later companies that claim a planet for $500 deposit + $50/month. They appear as orbiting planets in the outer ring. In gaming fiction: trade-route endpoints for the Hauling game mode.

4. **The Void** — everything unclaimed. Abandoned planets (inactive 180+ days) return to the void; their infrastructure remains but the orbit is reassigned. Derelict ships belong here. This is where PvE bosses live. In lore: the part of the universe that is not yet governed.

5. **The Board** — the rule-maker, internal. In gaming fiction, "the Board" is the shadowy council that approves planet sales and kicks out lawbreakers. Narrated in Quantum Live episodes.

### Player role
You are a **pilot** in Bob, the standard-issue starter ship. Your role is open — you choose what to do with your time:
- **Haul** cargo between planets, earn GHAI, upgrade Bob or buy a hauler-class ship
- **Race** in Speed Run tracks, earn cosmetic trophies and seasonal rewards
- **Explore** derelicts for salvage and the first card drops
- **Fight** PvE bosses for rare cards
- **Challenge** other pilots in PvP card battle inside the dome
- **Chat** in your ship with other pilots you pass in open space
- **Own** a planet (meta-game) — companies can claim, pioneers already have

There is no mandatory main quest. The universe is a sandbox with quest anchors.

### World rules (baked into gameplay)
1. **Bob is the only free ship.** Elite Dangerous principle — you always have Bob, you never lose Bob, but Bob is average in every stat. All other ships are earned, achievement-unlocked, or purchased with GHAI.
2. **You cannot be killed — you can be defeated.** There is no permadeath. A PvP loss returns you to Bob with no cargo. A PvE loss returns you to the nearest station.
3. **The dome rule.** PvP only happens inside an invisible dome. Outsiders can fly past and watch. No open-world ganking.
4. **Hauling is risk-free in friendly space.** No loot-stealing PvP during hauling runs. Hauling routes through contested space have optional risk modifiers (higher reward, chance of pirate encounter — PvE).
5. **Cards are space technology.** Every card represents a module, weapon, maneuver, or tactical AI that the ship loads. Lore frame: cards are "data shards" salvaged from derelicts or fabricated in stations.
6. **Planets are living NPCs.** A planet with high activity glows brighter in the galaxy. A planet ignored for 180+ days fades and returns to the void.

### The Bob backstory (proposed)
Bob is named by the Board. Every pilot starts in a ship called Bob — same hull, different paint. The joke is that "Bob" is the most-used placeholder in voidexa's code, so the Board decided every starter ship would be called Bob. Lore and inside-joke in one. Bob is affectionate.

### Enemies
- **Derelict AI** — hostile ship AIs that reboot when approached. First PvE boss category.
- **Rogue Agents** — NPC pilots representing the AI Cast's failure modes (a GPT-Rogue that refuses to stop, a Llama-Rogue that won't wake up). Mid-tier PvE.
- **The Silent Ones** — end-game lore enemies. No dialogue. Hardest PvE. Reserved for post-MVP.
- **Other players** in PvP dome — not "enemies" in lore, more "rivals" and "sparring partners."

### Universe scale rule
The galaxy must look good with 10 planets, 100 planets, and 500 planets. Rendering rules (inherited from Star System design):
- Distant planets are bright dots until zoomed
- Constellation clusters for related industries
- Search + directory sidebar always available
- No labeled planet soup — zoom reveals detail

## PART 3 — GAME MODES

Eight modes total. Three are MVP. The rest are post-MVP or already partially built.

### Mode 1 — SPEED RUN (MVP, build first)

**What it is.** Timed flight through a series of waypoint rings between planets. Player in Bob (or owned ship) races against their own best time or against a global leaderboard.

**How a player enters.** Click "Speed Run" on `/starmap` or in the shop tab. Selects a track. Loads into a pre-defined waypoint sequence.

**Core loop.** Start line → fly through 10–20 rings in order → finish line. Collision with ring = +0.5 s penalty. Missing a ring = must return and pass it.

**Win/loss state.** No loss. You finish or you give up. Best time saves to Supabase. Top 10 per track on a leaderboard.

**Progression.** New tracks unlock weekly. Seasonal cosmetic reward for top 100. Ship-class-specific leaderboards (Bob class, Fighter class, etc.) so a paid ship doesn't dominate Bob class.

**Monetization.** Free to play. Power-ups are in-game purchases:
- **Boost** (10 GHAI per charge) — 3 s of 2× speed, single use mid-race
- **Shield** (10 GHAI) — ignore one ring collision penalty
- **Ghost** (15 GHAI) — pass through one ring without needing to hit it exactly

These are the "Mario Kart style power-ups during free flight" idea. They don't guarantee a win — a clean run by a skilled pilot beats a sloppy run with three Boosts.

**Why this is the first mode.** Proves the 3D pipeline handles real gameplay. No card engine needed. No PvP infrastructure. Reuses the Vattalus cockpit, the free-flight controls, and the existing planet placements. Minimum new code: waypoint rings, timer, leaderboard table.

---

### Mode 2 — HAULING (MVP, build second)

**What it is.** Economy mode. Pick up cargo at Planet A, deliver to Planet B, earn GHAI. Different cargo types have different risk/reward profiles.

**How a player enters.** Fly up to any planet with a "Hauling Hub" marker. Interact with the hub. Browse available contracts. Accept one. Cargo appears in Bob's hold (visual indicator in HUD).

**Core loop.** Accept contract → fly to pickup planet → dock → collect cargo → fly to delivery planet → dock → drop off → receive GHAI.

**Win/loss state.** No PvP loss. PvE loss possible on high-risk contracts (pirate encounter, ship defeated returns you to nearest station with cargo lost). Low-risk contracts are safe.

**Progression.** Complete X hauling runs → unlock hauler-class ship blueprint. Complete Y runs → unlock "Certified Hauler" badge. Cargo types get more valuable as your hauling reputation grows.

**Ship roles unlock here.** Hauler-class has larger cargo capacity. Fighter-class is faster but small capacity. Explorer-class has scanner range to find rare cargo contracts. **This is where role differentiation becomes meaningful beyond PvP.**

**Monetization.**
- Free mode, earn GHAI
- Hauler-class ship: 2000 GHAI (or earned free through achievements)
- Cargo capacity upgrades: 100–500 GHAI per slot
- Fast-travel tokens to skip the flight (5 GHAI per use) — optional for grinders

**Why this is second.** Introduces the economy (earn → spend) without PvP risk. Proves the ship-role system. Gives players a reason to visit every planet.

### Mode 3 — PvE CARD BATTLE (MVP, build third)

**What it is.** Turn-based tactical combat. Player's ship + deck vs. boss ship + boss deck. Cards represent weapons, maneuvers, shields, drones, and tactical AI. The ship lives in the real 3D scene; the cards render as 2D panels *beneath* the ship.

**How a player enters.** Fly to a derelict (already spawned in Star System). Approach triggers a "Boarding detected — combat initiated" dialog. Player chooses their deck (or auto-deck). Enters battle scene.

**Core loop.** See PART 6 and PART 9 for full mechanics. Turn-based. Player plays 1–3 cards per turn. Boss plays scripted response. Cards resolve with 3D visual effects on ships.

**Win/loss state.**
- **Win:** Boss ship destroyed. Rewards: 1 random card drop, 50–500 GHAI, fragment of a rare card.
- **Loss:** Player ship destroyed. Return to nearest station. Deck intact. No GHAI lost. No cards lost.

**Progression.** 5 boss tiers. Each tier drops tier-appropriate cards. Completing all 5 tiers unlocks the weekly "Quantum Live Boss" slot where the Cast debate who is the hardest boss of the week.

**Monetization.**
- Free to play
- Card packs (5 cards, 1 rare guaranteed): 100 GHAI
- Cosmetic card sleeves: 50 GHAI
- Legendary ship with unique card synergy: 2000 GHAI
- Never sell win rate — only cosmetics and deck diversity

**Why this is third.** It is the highest-effort build. Turn engine, hand UI, targeting, resolve animations, matchmaking later for PvP — all start here. MVP ships with one boss, one tier, eight cards active. That proves the loop.

### Mode 4 — PvP CARD BATTLE (post-MVP, after PvE proves the engine)

**What it is.** Same turn-based card battle engine, two human players, matched by ship class and card pool rating.

**How a player enters.** On `/starmap` or from a planet, click "Find PvP." Enters matchmaking queue. When matched, both players' ships teleport to the nearest arena dome.

**The dome mechanic.** PvP only happens inside a transparent, invisible dome. Dome is rendered as a faint sphere at the arena location. Outsiders can fly past and see the two ships trading card plays through the dome — generates spectator interest. Combatants cannot leave the dome until the match resolves. Other players can observe but not interfere.

**Core loop.** Same as PvE but both players play cards. Response tension (see PART 9 for the reveal animation that matters).

**Win/loss state.**
- **Win:** +GHAI reward, +ranked rating, cosmetic drop.
- **Loss:** No GHAI loss, no card loss, small rating decrease. You still have Bob.

**Progression.** Ranked ladder. Seasonal cosmetic rewards at each rank tier. Weekly tournaments hosted by the Cast as Quantum Live content.

**Monetization.** Free entry. Battle Pass (500 GHAI one-time, 60 tier rewards over the season). Cosmetics.

## PART 4 — CARD ENTRY SYSTEM

### How a player gets a card

Seven entry paths. All converge into the player's card collection (Supabase `user_cards` table).

1. **Starter deck** — every new account gets 20 cards on first login (Bob-class basics)
2. **Derelict drop** — clear a PvE battle, 1 random card from that tier's loot table
3. **Card pack purchase** — 100 GHAI = 5 cards, 1 rare guaranteed. Monthly cap to prevent pay-to-win.
4. **Quest reward** — completing a quest arc drops a named card
5. **Pioneer reward** — pioneer planet owners get a unique "Pioneer" card series
6. **Achievement unlock** — long-term achievement unlocks (e.g., 100 hauling runs = "Certified Hauler" card)
7. **Crafting** — combine 3 duplicate commons → 1 random uncommon. Combine 3 uncommons → 1 rare. Caps prevent infinite crafting. 50 GHAI crafting fee per combine.

### Card anatomy

Every card has the following schema. Based on reviewing the 40 existing renders on `/cards` and reconciling with standard card-game practice:

| Field | Type | Example | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| `id` | uuid | — | Unique card instance (for crafting, fusing) |
| `template_id` | string | `pulse_shield_mk1` | The card type (40 templates initially) |
| `name` | string | "Pulse Shield Mk.1" | Display name |
| `type` | enum | `weapon` `defense` `maneuver` `drone` `ai` `consumable` | Determines resolve animation |
| `rarity` | enum | `common` `uncommon` `rare` `legendary` `pioneer` | Visual border color + drop rate |
| `cost` | int | `2` | Energy cost to play (see PART 6 for energy system) |
| `stats` | jsonb | `{"damage": 3, "range": "adjacent"}` | Type-specific fields |
| `ability_text` | string | "Deal 3 damage. If target is shielded, deal 1 extra." | Rules text |
| `flavor` | string | "Salvaged from a derelict Pioneer hauler." | Italic lore text |
| `art_url` | string | `/cards/pulse_shield_mk1.png` | Already exists for all 40 |
| `faction` | enum | `core` `pioneer` `void` `neutral` | Lore faction, minor gameplay modifier |
| `ship_class_restriction` | enum nullable | `hauler` `fighter` `explorer` or null | Null = playable by any ship |

### Deck construction rules
- Deck size: **20 cards**. Chosen because Marvel Snap's 12 is too thin for a 5-turn arc and Hearthstone's 30 is too long for the mobile-friendly feel voidexa wants.
- **Max 2 copies** of the same card template
- **Max 3 rare cards**, max 1 legendary
- Ship class restricts some cards: a Hauler-class card cannot be played from a Fighter-class ship
- Decks save to Supabase. A player can own multiple decks. Max 10 decks per account.

### Card slot into deck UX
- `/cards` page becomes `/cards` (gallery view) and `/cards/deck/:id` (deck builder)
- Left pane: collection browser with filter (type, rarity, faction, cost, owned-only)
- Right pane: current deck (20 slots visible)
- Drag-and-drop card from collection to deck slot
- Duplicate check enforced client + server side
- "Save deck" button writes to Supabase with optimistic lock for sync

## PART 5 — CARD EVALUATION AND BALANCE

### Evaluating the 40 existing cards

The renders are on `/cards`. Without seeing the exact art, I can build the evaluation framework for Claude Code to apply per-card once it reads the image filenames.

**Step 1 — classify by visual archetype.** For each render, answer three questions:
1. What does the card *look like*? (Weapon? Shield? Ship maneuver? Drone? Tactical AI? Enhancement?)
2. What is its scale? (Personal gear? Ship weapon? Fleet-level? Narrative?)
3. What faction does the art imply? (Core, Pioneer, Void, Neutral)

**Step 2 — assign a functional slot.** Map each render to one of:
- **Offense** (deals damage or disables)
- **Defense** (shields, evasion, repair)
- **Utility** (card draw, energy, scry, delay)
- **Control** (removes opponent's card, discards their hand, locks targeting)
- **Finisher** (high-cost, high-impact, ends games)

A healthy 40-card set needs roughly: 14 offense, 10 defense, 8 utility, 5 control, 3 finisher. Cards can serve multiple slots (a shield that reflects damage is defense + offense).

**Step 3 — cost and power curve.** Assign each card an energy cost (1–7) and a power rating at that cost. Plot the distribution. Flag outliers.

**Step 4 — ship-class restriction.** Some cards are obviously Hauler-type (cargo-related) or Fighter-type (offensive). Restrict them accordingly. Most cards are class-neutral.

**Step 5 — reworks.** Renders that do not fit any functional slot become **flavor cards** — legendary rare, unique ability that breaks a rule for a big effect. No more than 3 of the 40 should be pure flavor cards.

### Power-level evaluation (how we know a card is balanced)
Use the **2-cost damage baseline** method. A 2-cost card that deals 3 damage is the balance anchor. Everything is evaluated against this:
- 1-cost = half of the 2-cost baseline (usually 1–2 damage or a minor utility effect)
- 3-cost = 1.5× the baseline (4–5 damage or a medium effect)
- 5-cost = 2.5× the baseline (7–8 damage or a major effect)
- 7-cost finishers = 3×–4× the baseline (10–12 damage or game-ending board effects)

Any card that deviates more than 20% from its cost's baseline is flagged for rework unless the flavor text justifies a downside (e.g., "Deal 10 damage, discard your hand").

### Which card system fits voidexa best

**Comparison matrix** (dimensions: speed, depth, RNG, mobile-friendliness, dev cost):

| Game | Match length | Depth | RNG | Mobile UX | Dev cost | Fit for voidexa |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hearthstone | 10–20 min | Medium-high | High | Good | High | ❌ Too long, too much RNG |
| Magic: The Gathering | 20–40 min | Very high | Medium | Poor | Very high | ❌ Too complex for web-first |
| Gwent | 15–25 min | High | Low | Medium | Medium | ⚠️ Three-row board conflicts with 3D scene |
| Slay the Spire | 20–60 min (roguelike) | High | Medium | Medium | Medium | ✅ Perfect for PvE |
| Marvel Snap | 3–5 min | Medium | Medium | Excellent | Medium | ✅ Perfect for PvP |

**Verdict.** voidexa should run a **two-track design**:
- **PvE = Slay the Spire inspired.** Single-player roguelike-ish. Fly to derelict → 3–5 card battles → boss → rewards → return. Runs with relics (ship modules), act structure (derelict tiers 1–5), and a final boss per tier.
- **PvP = Marvel Snap inspired.** 3–5 minute matches. Fast turns. No overlong lock-in. Mobile-friendly. Inside-dome format with spectator visibility.

Both tracks share the same card templates, costs, and engine. Only the *match structure* differs. This is the key architectural decision — one card set, two match formats.

### Rarity distribution target
Of the 40 starter cards:
- **20 common** (50%) — base kit, new players see these first
- **12 uncommon** (30%) — drop from basic PvE, modest power
- **6 rare** (15%) — strong, identity-defining
- **2 legendary** (5%) — game-defining finishers
- **0 pioneer** at launch — pioneer cards are earned only by the first 10 planet owners and appear later

### Balance tooling (build in PART 12)
- `lib/game/balance.ts` — baseline calculator, cost-power curve checker
- Admin page `/control-plane/card-balance` — visualize distribution, flag outliers, propose reworks
- Live telemetry: per-card win-rate, per-card play-rate, per-card in-deck rate. Adjust monthly.

## PART 6 — DOES CARD PLAY WORK WITH THE CURRENT STACK

**Short answer: yes, without a rewrite. Additive.**

### The stack
- Next.js 16 on Vercel Pro (auto-deploy `git push origin main`)
- Three.js + React Three Fiber for 3D
- 689 `.glb` ship models on disk, ~60 on Supabase CDN
- 62 ship types, 425 skins, Hi-Rez assembly parts
- Supabase EU for persistence
- Vattalus cockpit live on `/freeflight`
- Phase 1–9 Star System done, 342 tests
- `/cards` page has 40 cards rendered, no combat
- Platform-GHAI $1 = 100 GHAI

### What already exists and can be reused
- **3D scene graph** (R3F `<Canvas>`) — reuse for battle scenes. Ships are already `.glb`-loadable.
- **Camera system** — already has cinematic focus transitions (used for Level 1 → Level 2 warp).
- **HUD overlay pattern** — Control Plane dashboard proves HTML over Canvas works in voidexa style.
- **Postprocessing** — `@react-three/postprocessing` is already in the repo for bloom, chromatic aberration. Reuse for ability flashes.
- **Supabase realtime** — already used in Void Chat. Reuse for PvP battle state sync.
- **Cockpit rendering** — Vattalus cockpit is now a known-good template.

### What needs to be added
Seven new pieces. None of them require breaking what exists.

1. **Turn state machine.** XState machine with states `awaiting_player_action` → `resolving_player_card` → `awaiting_opponent_action` → `resolving_opponent_card` → `checking_win_condition` → loop or end. Run in browser for single player; authoritative on server for PvP.

2. **Battle scene component.** `components/game/BattleScene.tsx` — an R3F scene with two ships (player bottom-near, opponent top-far), a starfield background, optional derelict debris, and bloom. Lightweight compared to the full star map.

3. **Card UI layer.** HTML + CSS + framer-motion over the Canvas. Hand at bottom, deck/discard on sides, energy bar, life total. Tailwind + shadcn components match existing voidexa style.

4. **Ability resolver.** `lib/game/resolve.ts` — pure function `(state, card, target) → newState + effectQueue`. The effectQueue drives the visual animations (PART 9). Pure-function approach means it is trivially testable.

5. **Matchmaking queue.** Supabase table `pvp_queue` with realtime subscription. Players insert a row with their ship class and rating; a serverless cron picks pairs.

6. **Battle persistence.** Supabase tables `battle_sessions`, `battle_turns`, `battle_cards_played`. PvE runs are stored for replay and telemetry.

7. **Asset loading strategy.** Ship `.glb` models lazy-load when a battle starts. Preload only the two ships involved. 6.8 GB total asset pool stays on Supabase CDN — we never ship all of it to the client.

### Three.js vs 2D canvas trade-off

| Dimension | Full Three.js battle scene | 2D canvas battle scene | Hybrid (recommended) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reuse of existing assets | High | Zero | High |
| Visual impact | High | Medium | High |
| Mobile performance | Medium (requires care) | Excellent | Good |
| Dev time | Medium-high | Medium | Medium |
| Feels like voidexa | Yes | No | Yes |

**Decision: Hybrid.** Three.js scene for ships + starfield + effects. HTML layer over the top for cards, hand, energy bar, life, turn indicator, buttons. The two layers share state via Zustand. This is the pattern PART 8 prescribes in full.

### Supabase fit

New tables needed (migrations in PART 12):
- `user_cards` — card instances owned
- `decks` — saved deck definitions
- `deck_cards` — join table (deck → card templates with copy count)
- `battle_sessions` — one per match, includes seed, deck snapshots, result
- `battle_turns` — per-turn log for replay
- `pvp_queue` — matchmaking
- `pvp_presence` — realtime presence (who is online, in-dome, spectating)
- `leaderboards` — aggregate rankings for PvE tiers, PvP rating, Speed Run times

All RLS-protected. Users read/write only their own `user_cards` and `decks`. Battles use service role for authoritative writes.

### Verdict
The engine can be built on top of what exists. No part of the current site gets rewritten. Card battle is additive, routed under `/game/battle/:session_id`, and the star map is untouched. MVP build effort: 4–6 Claude Code sessions (see PART 12).

## PART 7 — REFERENCES FROM OTHER CARD GAMES

What to take and what to reject, per reference.

### Hearthstone (Blizzard)
**Take:**
- Mana curve as a design discipline (cards cost 0–10 mana, total curve in a deck should be balanced)
- Hero power concept — in voidexa, this maps to a **ship class ability** usable once per turn for a low cost
- Visual effect polish on card play — the card physically leaves hand, rotates, impacts board

**Reject:**
- Random-target RNG cards that swing games (Yogg-Saron, Flamestrike-on-a-random-target) — voidexa should feel skill-dependent
- 30-card decks — too long for 3–5 minute PvP target
- The *hero* metaphor — voidexa uses the ship as the player avatar, not a portrait

### Magic: The Gathering (Wizards of the Coast)
**Take:**
- Color identity as a design principle — voidexa uses faction identity (Core = stable, Pioneer = high-reward, Void = high-risk, Neutral = flexible)
- Instant vs. sorcery distinction — voidexa has two speeds: **reactions** (played out-of-turn in response to opponent) and **actions** (played on your turn only)
- The **stack** as a way to resolve interactions — simplified version for voidexa: a one-slot response window after each card played

**Reject:**
- Mana types (five-color system) — too complex for web-first casual players
- Permanents vs. instants vs. sorceries distinction — too much for first-time players
- Land-drop mechanic — voidexa uses a simpler **energy per turn** system (starts at 1, +1 per turn, max 7)

### Gwent (CD Projekt Red)
**Take:**
- Best-of-three match structure for **tournament mode** (not default PvP)
- The "hand as resource" principle — discard a card to gain an advantage. Applied to voidexa as a "jettison cargo" mechanic: discard a card to reload energy
- Low-RNG, high-skill philosophy

**Reject:**
- Three-row battlefield — visually conflicts with the "ship in 3D space" aesthetic. voidexa battlefield is simply "near the opponent" or "near yourself", no rows.
- Passing your turn as a strategic choice — adds clarity cost, removes tempo pressure

### Slay the Spire (Mega Crit Games)
**Take:**
- Act structure for PvE — voidexa PvE is 3–5 battles per run, scaling difficulty, boss at the end
- Relics as persistent passive effects — in voidexa, these are **ship modules** equipped before the run
- Deck-building across a run — pick one of three card rewards after each battle
- Roguelike feel — losing a run resets the deck to starter for that specific run, but the meta-progression (card collection) persists

**Reject:**
- Single-player only mentality — voidexa needs the same cards to work in PvP too. Design discipline: **every card must make sense in both a 5-minute PvP match and a 40-minute PvE run.** That is a non-negotiable constraint.
- Permanent deck destruction between runs — voidexa's account collection persists; runs use a session-deck that resets

### Marvel Snap (Second Dinner)
**Take:**
- 3-minute match target for PvP
- Both players reveal cards simultaneously each turn — tension, surprise, speed
- Location modifiers — voidexa uses **arena modifiers** (e.g., "Nebula: all damage reduced by 1", "Derelict Wreck: +1 card draw per turn")
- Mobile-first UI design (we steal the feel; we render in Three.js)

**Reject:**
- The ladder system is grind-heavy — voidexa ladder resets seasonally with cosmetic rewards, not endless climb
- Power-on-location mechanic — visually conflicts with 3D ship combat

### Summary — what voidexa is
**voidexa card battle = Slay the Spire's single-player depth + Marvel Snap's PvP pace + Gwent's low-RNG philosophy + MTG's reactive-response mechanic + Hearthstone's polish on animations, all rendered inside the existing 3D ship universe.**

That is a specific, defensible design position.

### State flow

```
User clicks CardInHand
  → onPlay(card) dispatched to Zustand
  → turn machine transitions to "resolving_player_card"
  → resolve.ts runs: state × card × target → newState + effectQueue
  → Zustand updates: hand, energy, board, effectQueue
  → <AbilityEffects> reads effectQueue, renders 3D animation
  → <BattleHUDOverlay> reads state, updates life/energy bars
  → resolution completes → turn machine → "awaiting_opponent_action"
  → PvE: opponent AI picks next card via scripted logic
  → PvP: wait for Supabase Realtime broadcast of opponent move
  → repeat until win condition
```

### Zustand store shape

```ts
interface BattleState {
  // identity
  sessionId: string;
  mode: 'pve' | 'pvp' | 'practice';

  // players
  player: PlayerState;
  opponent: PlayerState;

  // turn
  turn: number;
  activePlayer: 'player' | 'opponent';
  phase: 'draw' | 'main' | 'resolve' | 'end';

  // animation queue
  effectQueue: AbilityEffect[];

  // log
  log: TurnLogEntry[];

  // actions
  playCard: (cardInstanceId: string, targetId?: string) => void;
  endTurn: () => void;
  concede: () => void;
}

interface PlayerState {
  deck: CardInstance[];
  hand: CardInstance[];
  discard: CardInstance[];
  shipGlbUrl: string;
  shipHull: number;      // life
  shipShields: number;   // regenerating second layer
  energy: number;
  maxEnergy: number;
  modifiers: Modifier[]; // buffs/debuffs
}
```

### XState turn machine

```
idle
  → match_start
    → draw_phase (player draws 1 card)
      → main_phase (player acts: play card, end turn, concede)
        → resolve_card (on play)
          → check_reactions (opponent may interrupt with reaction)
            → apply_effects
              → check_win (shipHull ≤ 0 for either)
                → (win) victory / (loss) defeat / (else) main_phase
        → (end turn)
        → opponent_turn
          → opponent_main
            → opponent_resolve
              → (loop to draw_phase after opponent ends)
```

### PvP sync via Supabase Realtime

Two players, two browsers. Authoritative battle state lives on Supabase via a serverless function, or in a lightweight lockstep model where each client broadcasts their move and both clients apply it deterministically (seed-based randomness).

Recommendation for MVP: **client-authoritative with server-side replay validation.** Each client runs the turn machine locally. Moves are broadcast via Supabase channel `battle:{sessionId}`. After each match, the server replays the turn log from the seed and validates the result. Cheaters caught on validation are shadowbanned.

This is the approach that lets a solo founder ship PvP in weeks, not quarters. Trade-off: clever cheaters may win a few matches before the replay catches them. Acceptable at voidexa's scale.

### Event bus pattern

Zustand store emits events through a tiny pubsub. The effectQueue is one consumer (drives 3D animation). The audio manager is another consumer (drives SFX). The battle log is a third consumer. Keeps concerns separated without prop-drilling.

#### State 3 — Ability trigger on the ship (3D scene)

```tsx
// components/game/AbilityEffects.tsx
'use client';
import { useRef, useEffect } from 'react';
import { useFrame } from '@react-three/fiber';
import { useBattleStore } from '@/hooks/game/useBattleStore';
import * as THREE from 'three';

const EFFECT_DURATION_MS = 900;

export function AbilityEffects() {
  const effectQueue = useBattleStore(s => s.effectQueue);
  const consumeEffect = useBattleStore(s => s.consumeEffect);
  const activeEffect = effectQueue[0];

  if (!activeEffect) return null;

  switch (activeEffect.kind) {
    case 'weapon_fire':
      return <WeaponFireEffect effect={activeEffect} onComplete={consumeEffect} />;
    case 'shield_up':
      return <ShieldEffect effect={activeEffect} onComplete={consumeEffect} />;
    case 'drone_deploy':
      return <DroneEffect effect={activeEffect} onComplete={consumeEffect} />;
    case 'repair':
      return <RepairEffect effect={activeEffect} onComplete={consumeEffect} />;
    case 'explosion':
      return <ExplosionEffect effect={activeEffect} onComplete={consumeEffect} />;
    default:
      return null;
  }
}

function WeaponFireEffect({ effect, onComplete }) {
  const meshRef = useRef<THREE.Mesh>(null);
  const startTime = useRef(performance.now());

  useFrame(() => {
    const elapsed = performance.now() - startTime.current;
    const t = elapsed / EFFECT_DURATION_MS;
    if (t >= 1) {
      onComplete();
      return;
    }
    if (meshRef.current) {
      // travel from attacker to target
      meshRef.current.position.lerpVectors(
        new THREE.Vector3(...effect.from),
        new THREE.Vector3(...effect.to),
        t
      );
      // shrink tail
      meshRef.current.scale.setScalar(1 - t * 0.5);
    }
  });

  return (
    <mesh ref={meshRef} position={effect.from}>
      <sphereGeometry args={[0.4, 16, 16]} />
      <meshBasicMaterial color="#7ffcff" toneMapped={false} />
      {/* trail */}
      <pointLight intensity={8} color="#7ffcff" distance={5} />
    </mesh>
  );
}

function ShieldEffect({ effect, onComplete }) {
  const meshRef = useRef<THREE.Mesh>(null);
  const startTime = useRef(performance.now());

  useFrame(() => {
    const elapsed = performance.now() - startTime.current;
    const t = elapsed / EFFECT_DURATION_MS;
    if (t >= 1) {
      onComplete();
      return;
    }
    if (meshRef.current) {
      const mat = meshRef.current.material as THREE.MeshBasicMaterial;
      mat.opacity = Math.sin(t * Math.PI) * 0.7;
      meshRef.current.scale.setScalar(2 + t * 0.5);
    }
  });

  return (
    <mesh ref={meshRef} position={effect.at}>
      <sphereGeometry args={[1.8, 32, 32]} />
      <meshBasicMaterial
        color="#7fd8ff"
        transparent
        opacity={0}
        side={THREE.DoubleSide}
        toneMapped={false}
      />
    </mesh>
  );
}

function ExplosionEffect({ effect, onComplete }) {
  // particle burst - use drei's Sparkles or a custom points system
  const pointsRef = useRef<THREE.Points>(null);
  const startTime = useRef(performance.now());

  useFrame(() => {
    const elapsed = performance.now() - startTime.current;
    const t = elapsed / (EFFECT_DURATION_MS * 1.3);
    if (t >= 1) {
      onComplete();
      return;
    }
    if (pointsRef.current) {
      pointsRef.current.scale.setScalar(1 + t * 4);
      const mat = pointsRef.current.material as THREE.PointsMaterial;
      mat.opacity = 1 - t;
    }
  });

  const particleCount = 80;
  const positions = new Float32Array(particleCount * 3);
  for (let i = 0; i < particleCount; i++) {
    const theta = Math.random() * Math.PI * 2;
    const phi = Math.acos(2 * Math.random() - 1);
    const r = 0.2 + Math.random() * 0.8;
    positions[i * 3] = r * Math.sin(phi) * Math.cos(theta);
    positions[i * 3 + 1] = r * Math.sin(phi) * Math.sin(theta);
    positions[i * 3 + 2] = r * Math.cos(phi);
  }

  return (
    <points ref={pointsRef} position={effect.at}>
      <bufferGeometry>
        <bufferAttribute
          attach="attributes-position"
          count={particleCount}
          array={positions}
          itemSize={3}
        />
      </bufferGeometry>
      <pointsMaterial
        size={0.12}
        color="#ff8a3c"
        transparent
        opacity={1}
        toneMapped={false}
        blending={THREE.AdditiveBlending}
      />
    </points>
  );
}
```

```tsx
// components/game/Ship.tsx (excerpt — only the hit-reaction part)
'use client';
import { useRef, useEffect } from 'react';
import { useGLTF } from '@react-three/drei';
import { useFrame } from '@react-three/fiber';
import * as THREE from 'three';
import { useBattleStore } from '@/hooks/game/useBattleStore';

export function Ship({ pos, glbUrl, side }) {
  const { scene } = useGLTF(glbUrl);
  const groupRef = useRef<THREE.Group>(null);
  const hitEffect = useBattleStore(s =>
    s.effectQueue.find(e => e.kind === 'hit_impact' && e.target === side)
  );
  const shakeRef = useRef({ startTime: 0, active: false });

  useEffect(() => {
    if (hitEffect && !shakeRef.current.active) {
      shakeRef.current = { startTime: performance.now(), active: true };
    }
  }, [hitEffect]);

  useFrame(() => {
    if (!groupRef.current) return;
    if (shakeRef.current.active) {
      const elapsed = performance.now() - shakeRef.current.startTime;
      const duration = 420;
      if (elapsed > duration) {
        shakeRef.current.active = false;
        groupRef.current.position.set(...pos);
      } else {
        const t = elapsed / duration;
        const amplitude = 0.18 * (1 - t);
        groupRef.current.position.set(
          pos[0] + (Math.random() - 0.5) * amplitude,
          pos[1] + (Math.random() - 0.5) * amplitude,
          pos[2] + (Math.random() - 0.5) * amplitude
        );
      }
    }
  });

  return (
    <group ref={groupRef} position={pos}>
      <primitive object={scene.clone()} />
      {/* Hull damage sparks overlay */}
      {hitEffect && <HullSparks intensity={hitEffect.damage} />}
    </group>
  );
}
```

```tsx
// components/game/BossIntroCinematic.tsx — runs once on PvE battle start
'use client';
import { useThree, useFrame } from '@react-three/fiber';
import { useRef, useEffect } from 'react';
import * as THREE from 'three';

export function BossIntroCinematic({ bossPosition, onComplete }) {
  const { camera } = useThree();
  const startTime = useRef(performance.now());
  const DURATION = 2400;

  useFrame(() => {
    const elapsed = performance.now() - startTime.current;
    const t = Math.min(elapsed / DURATION, 1);

    // ease-in-out cubic
    const eased = t < 0.5
      ? 4 * t * t * t
      : 1 - Math.pow(-2 * t + 2, 3) / 2;

    camera.position.lerpVectors(
      new THREE.Vector3(0, 5, 15),
      new THREE.Vector3(bossPosition[0], bossPosition[1] + 2, bossPosition[2] + 8),
      eased
    );
    camera.lookAt(new THREE.Vector3(...bossPosition));

    if (t >= 1) onComplete();
  });

  return null;
}
```

## PART 10 — QUEST AND STORYLINE SYSTEM

### Design principle
voidexa is a sandbox first, a story second. Players who want story get story; players who want speed-run leaderboards get those. Quests are **anchors**, not **rails**.

### The onboarding arc (Tutorial / First Hour)

Quest 1 — **"First Flight"** (3 minutes)
- You start in Bob at voidexa Core.
- Claude (French architect) hails you on voice: "Welcome, pilot. Let's check your controls."
- Fly through 3 waypoint rings near Core.
- Rewards: first Speed Run is unlocked, 100 GHAI.

Quest 2 — **"First Card"** (5 minutes)
- GPT-Commander hails: "Pilot, a derelict is drifting near Core. Recover it."
- Fly to the closest derelict.
- Approach triggers a **tutorial PvE battle** (scripted, one card each, cannot lose).
- Rewards: 1 random card, 50 GHAI, tutorial badge.

Quest 3 — **"First Haul"** (8 minutes)
- Perplexity (fact-checker): "Actually, Core needs supplies from Planet A. Take this contract."
- Accept a low-risk hauling contract.
- Pickup and deliver.
- Rewards: 150 GHAI, "Certified Pilot" badge, access to `/shop`.

Quest 4 — **"Choose Your Path"** (open-ended)
- Gemini (wise hippie): "The galaxy is patient. What calls to you?"
- Player is presented with three branches: Race (Speed Run track unlocked), Haul (hauling hub unlocked), Fight (first PvE tier unlocked).
- Each branch has its own mini-quest arc.

After Quest 4, the tutorial is over and the sandbox opens.

### The main arc (optional, unlocks at player level 10)

**Chapter 1 — The Silent Ones**
Perplexity flags an anomaly — derelicts are moving. Player investigates. Unlocks PvE tier 3–5 content. Ends with a boss fight against a "Silent Scout."

**Chapter 2 — The Board's Secret**
Quantum Live airs an episode about the Board's history. Claude admits he doesn't know what happened to the original voidexa founder. Player is asked to explore archived sectors. Rewards: unique story-only cards.

**Chapter 3 — The Pioneer Trail**
Player meets (NPC versions of) the First Ten pioneers. Each one gives a mini-quest. Completing all ten unlocks a legendary ship: "Founder's Wake" (cosmetic + small stat bonus).

**Chapter 4 — The Void's Offer**
A new faction emerges: The Void is offering contracts no one else will take. Players choose to accept or refuse. Moral choice affects which endgame cosmetics they unlock.

**Chapter 5 — Endgame (TBD, post-launch)**
Left open for player input. Quantum Live can run an actual audience vote on what happens in Chapter 5.

### Quest-to-gameplay mapping
- Quest trigger: player flies into a trigger zone, clicks an NPC, or accepts from a hub
- Quest objective types:
  - **Fly to X** — reuses free flight
  - **Win a battle at X** — reuses PvE engine
  - **Haul cargo between X and Y** — reuses hauling
  - **Beat Speed Run track X under time T** — reuses speed run
  - **Defeat a boss of tier N** — reuses PvE engine
  - **Chat with NPC X** — reuses AI chat with Cast persona
- Rewards:
  - GHAI (fixed amount)
  - One random card from a tier loot table
  - Named story-only card (legendary tier, unique)
  - Cosmetic badge
  - Lore log entry (readable on the player's profile)

### Where rewards come from (budget)
All GHAI rewards are minted on Supabase (platform-GHAI ledger). The total quest reward budget for the onboarding arc is capped at ~500 GHAI per player. The main arc is capped at ~5000 GHAI per player. This is a controlled sink, not an inflation spiral.

## PART 11 — DEVIATIONS AND OPEN QUESTIONS

Every place past discussion was contradictory, unfinished, or left hanging. One-line recommendation per item.

### Design-level deviations

1. **Is Bob the *only* free ship, or is there one free ship per role?**
   Recovered: "Bob is the only free ship." That is locked. Hauler, Explorer, Fighter class ships are all earned or purchased. **Recommendation:** keep Bob-only. Clarity over role-parity.

2. **Does hauling allow PvP interruption?**
   Contradiction in past notes: earlier hint of "contested space" implied PvP risk during hauling. Later notes said hauling is risk-free. **Recommendation:** hauling is always risk-free in friendly space; high-risk contracts exist with PvE encounters (pirates), not PvP. Never mix cargo with PvP loot-stealing.

3. **Card count per deck — 12, 20, 30?**
   Discussed in different contexts. **Recommendation:** 20. Resolved in PART 4.

4. **Cards beneath ship or cards instead of ship in battle?**
   Recovered intent: cards beneath ship (ship lives in the real world). **Recommendation:** confirmed. Hybrid engine in PART 8 implements this.

5. **Energy system name and cap.**
   Not discussed previously. **Recommendation:** call it "Power". Starts at 1 on turn 1, +1 per turn, caps at 7. Reloadable via specific cards.

6. **Turn timer in PvP — hard or soft?**
   Not discussed. **Recommendation:** 45-second soft timer per turn. After 45 s, a 15-s warning appears. After 60 s, the turn auto-ends. Prevents stalling without punishing slow thinkers.

7. **Does Bob have a PvP ranked ladder?**
   Uncertain. **Recommendation:** yes, but class-separated. Bob class has its own ladder so new players aren't matched against Legendary ships.

8. **Are achievement-unlocked ships tradable?**
   Not decided. **Recommendation:** no. Achievement ships are bound to the account. Prevents pay-for-achievement loopholes. Skins are account-bound too.

9. **Crafting failure rates?**
   Not decided. **Recommendation:** no failure. Combining 3 cards always produces one result. 50 GHAI crafting fee is the balance lever.

10. **Battle Pass — does it exist at launch?**
    Not decided. **Recommendation:** no. Launch with Speed Run + Hauling + PvE MVP first. Add Battle Pass at 3 months post-launch when playerbase is validated.

### Technical deviations

11. **XState vs hand-rolled state machine?**
    Not discussed. **Recommendation:** XState. Benefits outweigh the learning curve for a turn-based game. Pure function `resolve()` can still exist alongside.

12. **Ship model asset preloading — all 689 or on-demand?**
    Recovered: 689 on disk (6.8 GB), ~60 on Supabase. **Recommendation:** on-demand via Supabase CDN. Never ship the full pack to the client. Preload only the two ships involved in the current battle.

13. **Should PvP be authoritative server or client-authoritative with replay?**
    Uncertain. **Recommendation:** client-authoritative with replay validation (see PART 8). Ship fast, catch cheaters on replay.

14. **WebGL or WebGPU for the battle scene?**
    Not discussed. **Recommendation:** WebGL via R3F (current stack). WebGPU is not yet portable enough for a consumer product.

15. **Supabase Realtime for PvP — free tier limits?**
    Uncertain. **Recommendation:** check current Supabase Pro plan quotas. 500 concurrent connections is plenty for MVP. Upgrade to Team plan if launch exceeds that.

16. **Use of voidforge for card art generation?**
    Mentioned in the repo SKILL files. **Recommendation:** yes for future card expansions. The 40 existing renders are the launch set; future sets can be voidforge-assisted.

17. **Break Room 3D vs 2D panoramic?**
    Earlier: 2D interactive panorama with hotspots. Later: user said "making it more 3D." **Recommendation:** launch Break Room as 2D panorama (ChatGPT/Gemini-generated background + HTML hotspots). Upgrade to Three.js 3D room later.

18. **Ship tagger admin tool — does it belong here?**
    Built as a SKILL, waiting to run. **Recommendation:** it belongs to the ship catalog work, not gaming. Keep it out of gaming scope. Run it separately.

19. **Immersive HUD Option D (3D screens with data) — is this gaming or cockpit UI?**
    Bridges both. **Recommendation:** it's cockpit UI for free flight; the gaming layer reuses those screens to display battle HUD data during battle scenes. One implementation, two use cases.

20. **VoidCast / sitcom content vs Quantum Live — same thing or separate?**
    The Cast bible is shared. Quantum Live is the debate-show format; VoidCast is TikTok-style sitcom. **Recommendation:** same Cast, two output formats. Quantum Live is weekly, live, long-form.
