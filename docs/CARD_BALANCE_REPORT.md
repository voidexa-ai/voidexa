## 10. Simulation Limitations & Known Gaps


**AI-type card win rate gap:** AI cards average 45.0% win rate after patch, below the 50-52% target. This is a **simulation artifact**, not a card balance issue. The greedy AI in the simulator:
- Picks cards by immediate value (damage × 1.0, draw × 3.0)
- Cannot plan ahead (doesn't know that draw-2 finds a weapon next turn)
- Will always prefer a 6-damage weapon over a draw-2 AI card at the same cost
- In human play, AI cards that draw 2-3 and gain energy are premium picks because they enable combos and card advantage

**Recommendation:** Do NOT nerf AI cards to match the sim. Instead, when real player data arrives, compare human win rates against this baseline. AI cards that humans value highly but the sim undervalues confirm the sim's greedy bias.

**Other limitations:**
- Status effects (burn, jam, lock, expose) are partially valued — the sim doesn't track combo chains
- Mythic once-per-game effects are approximated, not enforced as exact game rules
- Hill-climbing converges to weapon-heavy decks because greedy AI plays them best
- No faction synergy modeled — pioneer/void faction bonuses would shift win rates

## 11. Recommendations for Next Steps


1. **Ship to production.** The card pool is significantly more balanced than pre-patch. 84% of cards land between 40-60% WR, zero dead cards, mythics properly tiered.
2. **Monitor real player data.** Track actual human deck compositions and win rates. The first 1,000 real matches will reveal whether AI-type cards are truly weak or just sim-disadvantaged.
3. **Consider nerfing remaining >60% weapons** if they dominate human play too: Unstable Warhead (64.1%), Rail Line (64.1%), Void Lance (63.3%).
4. **Overclock Core (34.6%)** remains the weakest card. It may need a complete redesign — consider making it a 0-cost 'free cantrip' that just draws 2.
5. **Add faction synergy** to the simulator in a future pass to test pioneer/void/core balance.
6. **Burst Window** (62.4% legendary maneuver) emerged as a new strong card post-patch — monitor and nerf if needed.
